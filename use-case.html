User Story

Peter wants to be able to go to one place to research and ID birds quickly and easily.

Use Case

While Peter is out bird watching he wants to be able to view pictures of birds and learn about their characteristics and habitat so he can identify the birds that he sees. Because he needs to make the most of his time, he needs a site that is fast and easy to use.

Usage Preconditions

User must have a search term to enter into the search box.

Usage Postconditions

The information that Peter wants is all in one place and is easily and quickly accessible on his iPhone X.

Interaction Flow

  • Peter clicks on the search button
  • The search pop-up box appears
  • Peter enters a species name into the search box and clicks Go
  • He is taken to the Overview page for the bird that he searched for
  • Peter views the main bird picture on the Overview page
  • He then clicks on a smaller picture located on the right-hand side of the site
  • He is taken to a library of more bird images and the picture he clicked on becomes the main picture
  • Peter clicks on another picture to view it and it appears as the main picture on the page
  • Peter clicks the Back button to go back to the Overview page
  • Peter is taken to the Overview page
  • Peter clicks on the Listen button to hear a sample of what the bird sounds like
  • The sound file of the bird plays
  • Peter clicks on the ID Info button and is taken to the ID Info page
  • He scrolls down to the Four Keys to ID section
  • He reviews Size & Shape, Color Pattern, Behavior, and Habitat
  • Peter scrolls back up to the top of the page
  • Peter clicks on Overview to go back to the Overview page
  • Peter is taken to the Overview page
  • Peter reviews the Basic Description, Find this Bird, Backyard Tips, and Cool Facts sections of the page
